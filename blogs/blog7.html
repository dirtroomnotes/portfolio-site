The Grit and Glory: How I Coded My Own Website (Without Being a Dev)

By Traci on September 1, 2025

The unemployment gap. It’s a phrase that can fill anyone with dread. For me, it became an unexpected runway. Facing job uncertainty, I channeled my energy into something ambitious and deeply personal: building my own website from scratch. And no, I didn’t just grab a WordPress theme or Wix template, I wanted full control, a unique digital home, and a challenge that would stretch my project management skills in a whole new way.

The catch? I wasn’t a developer. My background is in project management, focused on team dynamics, software execution, and strategy, not writing code. But leveraging lessons from past teams, I approached this project methodically: breaking down complex tasks, organizing my time and tools, and problem-solving. I learned a ton from resources like W3Schools, FreeFrontend, and others, using templates, code snippets, and layout examples to guide me. Some of the most painful challenges were implementing animations on the cards, and while I scrapped adding a divider/wave for now, I plan to bring it in later because I liked having that visual flourish. This website truly became my most personal and rewarding project yet.

My "Project Plan" for Learning to Code

  • HTML: The skeleton of the web.
  • CSS: Styling everything beautifully.
  • JavaScript: Adding interactivity.
  • Tailwind CSS: Rapid, responsive design without wrestling traditional CSS.
  • GitHub: Version control and backup.

The Gritty Reality: My Code Broke… A Lot

Let’s be honest: this wasn’t smooth sailing. I broke my code countless times, got tangled in messy CSS, and even scrapped my first version entirely. Starting fresh wasn’t as hard as the first attempt, since I had most of the fundamentals down. The real challenge was design and styling, deciding on colors, layouts, and overall aesthetics. I can be indecisive at times, so making sure everything looked and worked exactly how I envisioned was the kicker.

Applying PM Principles to Personal Development

I approached this project with a user-first mindset, just as I would for a client’s app. Every decision asked: Is it intuitive? Mobile-friendly? Useful?

  • Breaking Down the Work: Converted “build website” into small user stories
  • Iterative Development: Built, tested, broke, fixed, and refined
  • Scope Management: Defined a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and stuck to it.

The Payoff: More Than Just a Website

This site isn’t just a place to host my resume or portfolio. It’s a testament to lifelong learning and resourcefulness. Even without formal coding training, a project manager’s mindset like planning, iteration, and problem-solving, is transferable to any challenge.

My 6-Month Roadmap

  • Phase 1: Enhanced Usability & Navigation
    • Search functionality for projects and blog posts.
    • Filters and tags for easier browsing by category, technology, or topic.
  • Phase 2: User Experience Enhancements
    • Performance optimizations for speed and efficiency.
    • More dynamic, interactive elements to engage visitors.
“Even without formal coding experience, a project manager’s toolkit, planning, iteration, and problem-solving, can tackle any challenge.”

This phased approach allows me to continuously learn, build, and improve, much like a real-world software development project. It's an exciting journey, and I look forward to sharing these updates with you as they go live!
