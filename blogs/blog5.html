Understanding Web3: The Next Step in the Web

By Traci on August 22, 2025

The internet has transformed dramatically over the last few decades. From static pages to interactive platforms, and now toward intelligent, decentralized systems, the web keeps evolving. In my 2023 tech talk (link below), I explored the basics of Web3, a next-generation approach that’s fascinating, complex, and still very much a work in progress.

Web1: The Read-Only Web (1990–2000)

Web1 was the early internet: static, “read-only” pages where users could browse content but had very little ability to interact. Companies published information using HTML, hyperlinks, and simple forms. Users consumed content passively, and ownership rested entirely with the creators.

Web2: The Interactive Web (2000–Present)

Web2 brought interactivity. Users could create, share, and comment on content through blogs, social media, and web applications. Data ownership shifted toward platforms, and advertising became dynamic. While it connected billions, it also introduced challenges: privacy concerns, data breaches, and limited control over personal information.

Web3: The Decentralized, Intelligent Web

Web3 is a “read, write, and interact” internet emphasizing decentralization and user control. Leveraging blockchain, AI, and machine learning, it opens possibilities like:

  • AI-driven insights and semantic web capabilities
  • Blockchain, cryptocurrency, and NFTs
  • Smart contracts and decentralized governance (DAOs)
  • Decentralized apps (DApps) and storage solutions
  • Enhanced privacy and ownership over personal data

It’s exciting, but still early days. Current adoption is mostly in niche areas like crypto, NFTs, and experimental platforms. Challenges include scalability, user experience, security, and accessibility. Major shifts may take 10–15 years, so for most people, the web will feel familiar for a while.

Looking Ahead: Web4 & Web5

Discussions about Web4 and Web5 are already underway. These future iterations may bring fully intelligent, self-organizing systems and even deeper integration of AI, decentralized governance, and immersive experiences. While it sounds futuristic, understanding Web3 is the foundation for what’s coming.
